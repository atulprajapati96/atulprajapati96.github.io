Who I Am

About Me

I am a PostDoctoral Researcher at the University of L'Aquila, Italy, working on the XENONnT noble-liquid dark matter experiment and the DareDevil project — a low-mass dark matter search using GaAs crystal bolometers at LNGS.

I completed my Ph.D. at the Gran Sasso Science Institute (GSSI) in Astroparticle Physics, where my thesis focused on multivariate analysis and machine-learning-based background rejection for the CYGNO/INITIUM directional dark matter experiment.

I am a recipient of the Wenner-Gren Fellowship, and will be joining Stockholm University in July 2026 for my next PostDoc position, continuing work within the XENONnT collaboration.

Currently serving as Run Coordinator for XENONnT (since August 2025), and supervising a Master's thesis project on signal/background discrimination with ML in the XENONnT experiment.

What I Work On

Research Areas

⚫

Dark Matter Detection

Rare event searches with noble-liquid (XENONnT) and gaseous TPC (CYGNO/INITIUM) detectors. Directional sensitivity and low-mass dark matter with DareDevil/GaAs bolometers.

🤖

Machine Learning & CV

Mask R-CNN for 3D particle track reconstruction and identification. Deep neural networks, UNets, Res-UNets for signal/background discrimination in rare-event experiments.

🔭

Detector Physics & R&D

GEM amplification structures, negative ion drift at atmospheric pressure (first-ever optical readout), electroluminescence characterization, sCMOS & PMT readout systems.

⚛️

XENONnT Analysis

Peak bias correction maps using FUSE simulations, ¹³⁶Xe double-beta decay spectral modeling with BSM physics probes, data-taking and calibration operations at LNGS.

🛰️

Space Weather (Early Work)

Developed ANNIM-3D — an artificial neural network-based 3D ionospheric model trained on Ionosonde and GNSS-RO data for electron density and height prediction.

📊

Scientific Computing

ROOT, Geant4/FUSE simulations, Python ML pipelines, TensorFlow, OpenCV, parallel and GPU programming for high-performance physics data analysis.
